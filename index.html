What is a
Derivative?

Understand differentiation through the story of a moving car — before touching a single formula.

The Intuition

Three things that describe any motion

Imagine a car driving along a straight road. Every moment of its journey is captured by three connected ideas.

📍
Position

Where is it?

At any moment in time, the car is somewhere along the road. That location is its position. Our car's position grows as t².

s(t) = t²
💨
Velocity

How fast is it going?

Velocity is the rate of change of position. How quickly is the position updating each second? That's exactly what a derivative measures. The derivative of s(t) gives us velocity.

v(t) = 2t
🚀
Acceleration

Is it speeding up?

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity. Is the car going faster or slower? The derivative of velocity gives us acceleration — here it's a constant 2, meaning the car speeds up steadily.

a(t) = 2
The Visual

Watch the derivatives build themselves

The red tangent line shows the slope of the curve at each instant. That slope is the derivative — and it becomes the value plotted on the next graph.

The Mathematics

The chain of derivatives

Each arrow means: find the rate of change. That's all differentiation is.

Position s(t) = t²
→ d/dt
Velocity v(t) = 2t
→ d/dt
Acceleration a(t) = 2

The position graph curves upward · the velocity graph is a straight line · the acceleration is flat.
Each shape is exactly what the maths predicts — and exactly what you saw in the animation.
