What you're seeing
Matrix = movement rules.
Columns tell you where î and ĵ land. Every point follows automatically.

Determinant = area scaling.
Watch the green unit square. Its area = the determinant. Zero means space collapsed — no inverse.

Negative det = space flipped.
Orientation reverses. Like turning the chessboard over.

Identity = do nothing.
î stays (1,0), ĵ stays (0,1). Every point stays put.
Try this
1. Hit Collapse — grid squishes to a line. Det → 0.

2. Hit Rotate 90° — grid spins. Det stays 1. Area preserved.

3. Hit Reflect — det goes negative. Grid flips.

4. Drag sliders slowly and watch the determinant bar move live.
